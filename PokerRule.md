## 1. Overview

Texas Hold'em is the most widely played variant of poker. The goal is to make the best possible **5-card hand** using any combination of your 2 private hole cards and 5 shared community cards on the board.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Players | 2 – 9 per table |
| Deck | Standard 52-card deck (no Jokers) |
| Objective | Win chips by having the best hand at showdown, or by forcing all other players to fold |
| Game Flow | Pre-Flop → Flop → Turn → River → Showdown |

---

## 2. Table Setup & Positions

### 2.1 The Dealer Button

- A round token marked **"D"** (Dealer Button) designates the nominal dealer for the hand.
- The button moves **one position clockwise** after each hand.
- In a casino or online game, a professional dealer deals the cards; the button only determines betting order.

### 2.2 Seating Positions

Positions are named relative to the Dealer Button (clockwise):

| Position | Short Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Dealer (Button) | BTN | Last to act in post-flop rounds; strongest position |
| Small Blind | SB | First position left of BTN; posts the small blind |
| Big Blind | BB | Second position left of BTN; posts the big blind |
| Under the Gun | UTG | Third position left of BTN; first to act pre-flop |
| UTG+1, UTG+2 | — | Positions after UTG (in 6+ player games) |
| Middle Position | MP | Middle seats |
| Hijack | HJ | Two seats right of BTN |
| Cutoff | CO | One seat right of BTN |

> **Acting Order (Pre-Flop):** UTG → UTG+1 → … → CO → BTN → SB → BB  
> **Acting Order (Post-Flop):** SB → BB → UTG → … → BTN

### 2.3 Heads-Up (2 Players)

In a two-player game:
- The Dealer Button posts the **Small Blind** and acts **first pre-flop**.
- The non-button player posts the **Big Blind** and acts **last pre-flop**.
- Post-flop: the non-button player acts first.

---

## 3. Card Dealing

1. The dealer shuffles the 52-card deck thoroughly.
2. Starting with the player to the left of the button, the dealer deals cards **one at a time, clockwise**.
3. Each player receives **2 private hole cards** (face down), dealt in two rounds.
4. Over the course of the hand, **5 community cards** are dealt face up in the center of the table:
   - **Flop**: 3 cards at once
   - **Turn**: 1 card
   - **River**: 1 card
5. A **burn card** (discarded face-down card) is dealt before the Flop, Turn, and River.

---

## 4. Blinds & Antes

### 4.1 Small Blind (SB)

- Posted by the player **immediately left of the Dealer Button**.
- Amount is typically **half** of the Big Blind (e.g., SB = 5, BB = 10).
- The SB is a **forced bet** placed before any cards are dealt.

### 4.2 Big Blind (BB)

- Posted by the player **two seats left of the Dealer Button**.
- The BB is the **minimum bet size** for the pre-flop round.
- If no one raises, the BB may **check or raise** when the action returns to them (the "option").

### 4.3 Ante (Optional)

- An ante is an additional **forced bet** posted by every player (or sometimes only the BB) before cards are dealt.
- Common in tournament play to increase action.
- Antes go directly into the pot before the hand starts.

### 4.4 Blind Levels

In tournaments, blinds increase at fixed intervals (e.g., every 15–20 minutes). In cash games, blinds are fixed throughout.

## 5. Betting Rounds

Each hand consists of up to four betting rounds. Chips wagered each round are collected into the **pot**.

### 5.1 Pre-Flop

- Triggered after each player receives their 2 hole cards.
- Action begins with the player to the **left of the Big Blind (UTG)** and moves clockwise.
- The BB has already posted a bet equal to the big blind; all other players must at least **Call** the BB, **Raise**, or **Fold**.
- When action returns to the BB:
  - If no one has raised: the BB may **Check** (take the "option") or **Raise**.
  - If someone has raised: the BB must **Call**, **Re-raise**, or **Fold**.

### 5.2 Flop

- The dealer burns 1 card, then deals **3 community cards** face up.
- Action begins with the **first active player left of the Dealer Button** (i.e., SB or next active player).
- Since no bet has been placed yet this round, players may **Check** or **Bet**.

### 5.3 Turn

- The dealer burns 1 card, then deals **1 community card** face up (the 4th board card).
- Same acting order as the Flop.
- In **Limit Hold'em**, the bet size doubles on the Turn (not relevant in No-Limit).

### 5.4 River

- The dealer burns 1 card, then deals the **final community card** face up (the 5th board card).
- Same acting order as the Flop and Turn.
- If more than one player remains after betting, the hand proceeds to **Showdown**.

### 5.5 End of a Betting Round

A betting round ends when:
- All players have acted at least once **and** all bets/raises have been called (no outstanding action), **or**
- All players except one have folded.

## 6. Player Actions

When it is a player's turn to act, they must choose one of the following actions:

---

### 6.1 Fold

**Definition:** Discard your hole cards and forfeit any claim to the current pot.

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| When available | Any time it is your turn to act |
| Cost | None (but you lose any chips already committed to the pot) |
| Effect | You are eliminated from the current hand; you cannot win the pot |

**Example:** Pre-flop, UTG raises to 30. You hold 7♣ 2♦. You fold — discarding your cards face-down.

---

### 6.2 Check

**Definition:** Decline to bet, passing the action to the next player without putting in chips.

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| When available | Only when **no bet** has been placed in the current round, or you have already matched all bets |
| Cost | 0 chips |
| Effect | Action passes clockwise; you remain in the hand |

> **Note:** Checking is **not available** pre-flop (the BB is already a live bet), except for the BB if no one has raised.

**Example:** On the Flop, the SB checks. You are in the BB position with no bet to call — you may check.

---

### 6.3 Call

**Definition:** Match the current highest bet or raise to remain in the hand.

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| When available | When a bet or raise has been placed and you wish to continue |
| Amount | Equal to the **current highest bet** minus any chips you have already put in this round |
| Effect | You stay in the hand; action continues to next player |

**Calling the Big Blind (Limp):** Pre-flop, if no one has raised, calling the BB amount is called **limping in**.

**Example:** BB is 10. UTG raises to 30. You call 30. The next player must now call 30 (or raise/fold).

---

### 6.4 Bet

**Definition:** Place the first wager of a betting round (only available when no one has bet yet).

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| When available | When **no bet** has been made in the current round (post-flop only) |
| Minimum amount | Equal to the **Big Blind** (in No-Limit Hold'em) |
| Maximum amount | All your remaining chips (in No-Limit Hold'em) |
| Effect | Sets the price to call for all remaining players |

**Example:** On the Turn, everyone checks to you. You bet 50. All remaining players must call 50, raise, or fold.

---

### 6.5 Raise

**Definition:** Increase the current bet amount, forcing other players to call the higher amount, re-raise, or fold.

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| When available | When a bet or raise has been placed in the current round |
| Minimum raise | The raise amount must be **at least equal to the previous raise or bet size** (No-Limit Hold'em rule) |
| Maximum raise | All remaining chips (in No-Limit Hold'em) |
| Effect | All players who have already called must act again (call the difference, re-raise, or fold) |

**Minimum Raise Rule:**
- If the previous bet was 20, the minimum raise is to 40 (raise by at least 20).
- If someone raises from 20 to 60 (a raise of 40), the next raise must be to at least 100 (raising by at least 40).

**Example:** BB is 10. UTG raises to 30 (a raise of 20). You re-raise to 70 (a raise of 40, which is ≥ the previous raise of 20). ✓

---

### 6.6 Re-Raise (3-Bet, 4-Bet, …)

Re-raising terminology by number of bets:

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Open Raise (2-Bet) | First raise of the round (over the BB or a check) |
| 3-Bet | Re-raise over an open raise |
| 4-Bet | Re-raise over a 3-bet |
| 5-Bet / Shove | Typically an all-in re-raise |

The same minimum raise rule applies at every level.

---

### 6.7 All-In

**Definition:** Bet or call with **all remaining chips**, going all-in.

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| When available | Any time it is your turn to act |
| Amount | All remaining chips in your stack |
| Effect | You are eligible to win chips up to your all-in amount from each other player (main pot); any excess goes into a side pot you cannot win |

**All-In Less Than Minimum Raise:**
- If a player goes all-in for an amount less than the minimum raise, this does **not** reopen the betting for players who have already called.
- Example: Player A bets 100. Player B goes all-in for 140 (only 40 more, less than the 100 minimum re-raise). Player C, who already called 100, does **not** get to re-raise — they may only call 40 or fold.

**All-In with a Short Stack:**
- A player may call a bet even if they cannot cover the full amount. They go all-in for their remaining chips and are eligible to win only the amount they matched from each opponent.

## 7. Hand Rankings

Hands are ranked from highest to lowest. The best 5-card combination wins.

| Rank | Hand | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Royal Flush** | A-K-Q-J-10 of the same suit | A♠ K♠ Q♠ J♠ 10♠ |
| 2 | **Straight Flush** | Five consecutive cards of the same suit | 9♥ 8♥ 7♥ 6♥ 5♥ |
| 3 | **Four of a Kind** | Four cards of the same rank | K♣ K♦ K♥ K♠ 7♦ |
| 4 | **Full House** | Three of a kind + a pair | Q♠ Q♦ Q♥ 9♣ 9♦ |
| 5 | **Flush** | Five cards of the same suit (not sequential) | A♦ J♦ 8♦ 5♦ 2♦ |
| 6 | **Straight** | Five consecutive cards (any suit) | 8♣ 7♦ 6♥ 5♠ 4♣ |
| 7 | **Three of a Kind** | Three cards of the same rank | J♠ J♦ J♣ 9♥ 4♣ |
| 8 | **Two Pair** | Two different pairs | A♠ A♦ K♥ K♣ 7♠ |
| 9 | **One Pair** | Two cards of the same rank | 10♠ 10♦ A♣ K♥ 5♦ |
| 10 | **High Card** | No combination; highest card plays | A♠ Q♦ 9♣ 6♥ 2♠ |

### 7.1 Tiebreakers (Kickers)

When two players have the same hand type, the **kicker** (the next highest unpaired card) determines the winner.

**Examples:**
- Both players have a pair of Aces: A-A-**K**-7-3 beats A-A-**Q**-7-3 (King kicker beats Queen kicker).
- Both players have Q-Q-J-J: the fifth card (kicker) decides.
- Identical 5-card hands → **split pot** (chop).

### 7.2 Using Hole Cards

Players may use:
- **Both** hole cards + 3 community cards
- **One** hole card + 4 community cards
- **Zero** hole cards + 5 community cards (play the board)

> In standard Texas Hold'em, there is no requirement to use a minimum number of hole cards (unlike Omaha).

### 7.3 The Wheel (Ace-Low Straight)

- A-2-3-4-5 is a valid straight (the lowest possible straight), called **"the wheel"**.
- In this case, the Ace acts as a **low card (value 1)**.
- A-2-3-4-5 loses to 2-3-4-5-6.

## 8. Showdown

### 8.1 When Showdown Occurs

A showdown happens when **two or more players remain** after all betting on the River is complete.

### 8.2 Showdown Order

1. The **last aggressor** (the player who placed the last bet or raise) must show their cards first.
2. If no bet was made on the River, the player **closest to the left of the Dealer Button** shows first.
3. Remaining players show clockwise.
4. A player may **muck** (discard face-down) their hand if they cannot beat the shown hand — however, they forfeit any claim to the pot.

### 8.3 Best Hand Wins

- Each player uses any combination of their 2 hole cards and the 5 community cards to make their best 5-card hand.
- The player with the highest-ranked hand wins the pot.
- If hands are equal, the pot is **split** equally (chop pot).

### 8.4 Cards Speak

In a showdown, **"cards speak"** — the dealer (or system) reads all exposed hands and awards the pot to the best hand, even if a player misreads their own hand.

## 9. Pot Distribution

### 9.1 Main Pot

The main pot contains chips from all players up to the amount of the **smallest all-in** among the all-in players.

### 9.2 Side Pot

When a player is all-in for less than the full bet, a side pot is created:

- The **side pot** contains only the additional chips from players who can still cover the full bet.
- All-in players are **not eligible** to win side pots they did not contribute to.

**Example:**

| Player | Stack | All-In Amount |
|---|---|---|
| A | 50 (all-in) | 50 |
| B | 200 | 50 (main) + 150 (side) |
| C | 200 | 50 (main) + 150 (side) |

- **Main pot:** 50 × 3 = 150 (A, B, C all eligible)
- **Side pot:** 150 × 2 = 300 (B and C only)

If A has the best hand among all three → A wins the main pot (150).  
B and C then compare for the side pot (300).

### 9.3 Multiple All-Ins

If multiple players are all-in for different amounts, multiple side pots are created, one for each all-in level.

### 9.4 Ties (Chop Pot)

- If two or more players have **identical best 5-card hands**, the pot is split equally.
- Odd chips that cannot be split evenly go to the player **closest to the left of the Dealer Button**.

## 10. Special Situations

### 10.1 The Big Blind Option (Pre-Flop)

If no player has raised pre-flop (everyone just called or folded), the BB has the **"option"** — they may raise even though no one raised before them. This is because their forced BB bet counts as their bet for the round.

### 10.2 String Bet

A **string bet** is illegal. A player must announce the full raise amount before placing chips, or place all chips at once. You cannot put in chips, see a reaction, and then add more chips.

**Enforcement:** Only the initial amount (the call portion) is counted; the additional chips are returned.

### 10.3 Betting Cap (Limit Hold'em)

In **Limit Hold'em**, bets and raises are fixed:
- Pre-Flop and Flop: bets/raises in **small bet** increments.
- Turn and River: bets/raises in **big bet** increments (double).
- Maximum **3–4 raises** per round (cap), except heads-up.

> This document primarily covers **No-Limit Hold'em**, where any amount up to all-in can be bet/raised.

### 10.4 Misdeal

A misdeal is declared when dealing errors occur (e.g., a card exposed during the deal before all players receive their hands). The deck is reshuffled and hands are re-dealt. The button does **not** move.

### 10.5 Dead Button

If a player is absent or eliminated and the button position has no active player, the button still advances normally (it becomes a **dead button**). Blinds are posted by the next active players in order.

### 10.6 Running It Multiple Times

By agreement (common in cash games), if a player is all-in before the River, the remaining community cards may be dealt **twice or more** (each time from a reshuffled remainder of the deck). The pot is then split proportionally based on wins across the runs.

### 10.7 Rabbit Hunting

After a hand ends (either by folds or showdown), some tables allow **rabbit hunting** — revealing what community cards would have come next. This is for curiosity only and has no effect on the result.

### 10.8 Table Stakes Rule

A player can only wager chips **currently on the table** in front of them. You cannot reach into your pocket for more chips mid-hand. Additional chips can only be added between hands.

## Quick Reference: Player Actions Summary

| Action | Available When | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fold** | Any time | 0 (lose pot equity) | Exit current hand |
| **Check** | No bet in current round | 0 | Pass action; stay in hand |
| **Call** | Bet/raise exists | Match current bet | Stay in hand |
| **Bet** | No bet in current round (post-flop) | Min: 1 BB | Set price for others |
| **Raise** | A bet exists | Min: previous bet × 2 (or +prev raise size) | Force others to act again |
| **All-In** | Any time | All remaining chips | Win up to matched amount |

## Appendix: Round-by-Round Flow

```
HAND START
│
├─ Blinds posted (SB, BB)
├─ Antes posted (if applicable)
│
├─ [PRE-FLOP]
│   ├─ 2 hole cards dealt to each player
│   ├─ Action: UTG → ... → BTN → SB → BB
│   └─ Actions available: Fold / Call / Raise / All-In
│       (BB may Check if no raise)
│
├─ [FLOP]
│   ├─ Burn 1 card; deal 3 community cards face up
│   ├─ Action: SB → BB → ... → BTN
│   └─ Actions available: Fold / Check / Bet / Call / Raise / All-In
│
├─ [TURN]
│   ├─ Burn 1 card; deal 1 community card face up
│   ├─ Action: SB → BB → ... → BTN
│   └─ Actions available: Fold / Check / Bet / Call / Raise / All-In
│
├─ [RIVER]
│   ├─ Burn 1 card; deal 1 community card face up
│   ├─ Action: SB → BB → ... → BTN
│   └─ Actions available: Fold / Check / Bet / Call / Raise / All-In
│
└─ [SHOWDOWN] (if 2+ players remain)
    ├─ Last aggressor shows first
    ├─ Best 5-card hand wins
    └─ Pot awarded; button moves clockwise
```
